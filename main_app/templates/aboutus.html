OUR HISTORY

From Tragedy To Global Activism

Following the tragic 2010 earthquake in Haiti, Jean Paul Laurent, then a student at NYU’s School of Dentistry, returned to his native country to provide free dental care and supplies to a camp. While there, he was moved by the unwavering smiles of young children as they played amid the rubble and devastation around them–many of whom were suffering from severe tooth decay. Looking at these children, Laurent vowed to preserve the quality and health of their smiles and those of all disadvantaged children around the world–and the Unspoken Smiles was born. Since our founding, we have grown from a pilot project in Haiti into a global movement, serving over 10,000 children in 9 countries across 4 continents.

OUR VISION FOR CHANGE

According to a new Global Oral Health Status Report published on november 21, 2022 by the World Health Organization (WHO), it is estimated that oral diseases affect more than 530 million children worldwide, directly affecting their school attendance, social interaction, and overall health. Oftentimes, these oral diseases are completely avoidable, such as untreated tooth decay, but are left overlooked, as oral disease is considered the fourth most expensive disease to treat.

Unspoken Smiles recognizes the lack of access to oral healthcare as one of the most under-publicized, and simultaneously one of the most detrimental, factors limiting the full potential of children in low-income areas. With a focus only on preventative care, we will installed community dental clinics throughout the Americas that will provide critical primary care services at substantial savings creating unparalleled access to preventative care.





HOW WE WORK

What further provides Unspoken Smiles with a strong competitive advantage is our multi-prong approach that includes education programs that will be effective in driving a grass roots behavior change and healthy oral habits for a lifetime. In addition, Unspoken Smiles will also provide attractive fellowships for mid-career level training creating economic opportunities and tackling root causes such as women empowerment and economic development.
